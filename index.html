The development of prostate cancer occurs when abnormal cells start developing in the prostate,
a walnut-shaped gland beneath the bladder in males or those who were born male.
The prostate is generally around 4 cm across, but it grows larger with age.
Depending on whether the tumour is determined to be benign or malignant,
doctors may be concerned about any aberrant cell proliferation in the prostate.Learn more...

Some of the prostate cancer symptoms are:

  • Urine with blood in it could appear pink, crimson, or cola-colored.
  • Semen with blood in it.
  • Requiring more frequent urination.
  • Difficulty starting the urination process.
  • Increasing the frequency of nighttime urination awakenings.Learn more...
